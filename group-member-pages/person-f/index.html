Introduction

Hello! My name is Nolan, and I'm not sure what my career will be specifically, but what I do know is that I've always wanted to write.

My writing journey has taken me many places. Time travel novels. Award-winning newspapers. Blog writing. Creative writing classes. The list goes on and on. On the site, I'd like to feature some of the writing I've done over the years, as well as excerpts from them. Hope you enjoy!

Excerpt: The Prophecy

"A death will unleash the archer's wrath, sending both friend and foe to death's path.''

I came up with the idea for the story in 7th, in the middle of standardized testing. My first draft ended up the writing submission section of the test. I had written shorter forms of the story sas a second draft, but this is when I committed to doing a full length story. I planned to make it a five book series, and I am not even done with the second one, which has been in progress for five years. The pandemic was actually good timing for this story, because the extra time at home allowed me to buckle down and finish the story. I am considering revising it at some point, but for now, I'm keeping it as it is as a reminded of the early days of my creative writing journey.

Daily Californian Higher Education Beat Reporter

Contributed stories from 2023 to 2025. Below is an article of mine that made the front page of the weekly physical newspaper.

Article Link

My time with the Daily Californian built upon experience gained from my journalism class in high school. There, I was the Co-Editor-In-Chief of our newspaper during junior and senior year of high school. Daily Cal was an adjustment because while in high school we would have a few weeks to do a story, the turnaround time, from recieving your story to publication, was less than 24 hours. it was good experience in efficiency, and how to effectively reach out to sources and get quotes in a timely manner. The fact-checking process gave me a taste of what professionalal newsrooms are like in terms of informaiton verification.

UC Berkeley Creative Writing Minor

I have taken two creative writing classes for the minor, one last spring and the other this spring. Below is my most recent project for the short fiction class: the first draft of my short story called: "June 22, 2025"

Eng 143A Short Story - "June 22, 2025"

Ending Excerpt from "June 22, 2025":

"As I take in the static, Death has never felt stronger. I feel something end. What did I do wrong? I grab my pendant, and shift it around some more, but the compass provides no answers. Barely aware of a starting engine outside, I stumble over to the window just in time to see them leaving in Jackson’s dusty Toyota as he swerves and speeds recklessly away. They disappear southwards into the night, never to be seen by me again. I look down at the pendant, now damp from the sweat of my palm. The compass points north.''

After writing a fully-fledged short story and a few poems in my previous creative writing class, it's been really fun to focus soley on short stories. Unless I go back to my time travel story, my most likely form of fiction I'll be working on in my spare time is short-form fiction like this. What's been really helpful is reading the work of the other students and giving them feedback. My story is last to be reviewed, so I'm very excited to get comments from my classmates this Thursday!
